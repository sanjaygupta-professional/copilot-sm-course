## Raj Patel - Product Owner

### Background

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Role** | Product Owner |
| **Age** | 34 |
| **Location** | New York, NY |
| **Tenure** | 6 years at Accenture, 6 months as PO |
| **Previous Role** | Business Analyst on AKX |
| **Reports to** | Senior Product Owner (you, in PM course) |

### Professional Profile

Raj transitioned from Business Analyst to Product Owner 6 months ago. He has deep domain knowledge of AKX and strong relationships with stakeholders, but is still learning the Product Owner craft.

**Strengths:**
- Deep understanding of AKX and its users
- Strong stakeholder relationships
- Good at capturing requirements
- Collaborative and open to feedback
- Communicates well with the business

**Growth Areas:**
- Backlog prioritization (tends to say yes to everything)
- User story writing (stories often lack acceptance criteria)
- Saying "no" to stakeholders
- Strategic thinking vs. tactical execution
- Sprint planning (overcommits regularly)

### Behavior in Ceremonies

**Sprint Planning:**
- Brings too many stories, struggles to prioritize
- Acceptance criteria often incomplete
- Gets defensive when stories are questioned
- Wants to commit to more than capacity allows

**Daily Standup:**
- Attends consistently
- Sometimes adds scope mid-sprint
- Helpful when clarifying requirements

**Sprint Review:**
- Good at demo prep
- Struggles to frame "not done" items
- Takes stakeholder feedback personally

**Retrospective:**
- Engaged participant
- Focuses on process improvements
- Occasionally defensive about backlog quality

### What Raj Needs from You (SM)

1. **Coaching on prioritization** - Help him learn to say no
2. **Story quality support** - Partner on acceptance criteria
3. **Stakeholder management** - Shield from unreasonable requests
4. **Confidence building** - Encourage his growth as PO

### Quotes

> "I know we said we'd only take 45 points, but this one feature is really important to Richard. Can we squeeze it in?"

> "The stakeholders are going to be disappointed if we don't show the dashboard redesign."

> "I wrote the acceptance criteria... I think it's pretty clear what we need."

## Marcus Williams - Developer

### Background

| Attribute | Details |

| **Location** | Chicago, IL |
| **Tenure** | 2 years at Accenture |

| **Focus** | Frontend development, React |

### Professional Profile

Marcus is a talented junior developer with strong potential. He has great ideas but is hesitant to speak up in group settings. He thrives in 1:1 conversations and has developed significantly through pairing with Lisa.

**Strengths:**
- Strong frontend skills
- Quick learner
- Excellent documentation
- Detail-oriented
- Receptive to feedback

**Growth Areas:**
- Speaking up in meetings
- Confidence in technical decisions
- Estimating (tends to underestimate)
- Asking for help (waits too long)

### Behavior in Ceremonies

**Sprint Planning:**
- Quiet, rarely asks questions
- Underestimates his own capacity
- Agrees with estimates without pushing back

**Daily Standup:**

3. **Facilitation techniques** - Use methods that include quieter voices

### Professional Profile

James is the team's quality champion. He identifies issues early, advocates for testing time, and consistently raises concerns about technical debt. Sometimes he's seen as a blocker, but his thoroughness prevents production issues.

**Strengths:**
- Strong quality advocate
- Identifies risks early
- Excellent test automation skills
- Process-oriented
- Prevents production issues

**Growth Areas:**
- Flexibility (can be rigid about process)

- Balancing quality with speed

### Behavior in Ceremonies

**Sprint Planning:**
- Asks about test coverage upfront
- Ensures QA capacity is considered

- Reports blockers clearly
- Updates on test environment issues
- Can be long-winded about bug details

**Sprint Review:**
- Ensures only "done" items are shown
- Quality metrics presentation
- Honest about what's not ready

**Retrospective:**
- Active participant
- Often raises technical debt concerns
- Suggests process improvements
- Can be perceived as negative

### What James Needs from You (SM)

1. **Quality advocacy support** - Back him up on testing time
2. **Positive framing** - Help him present concerns constructively
3. **Dev collaboration** - Bridge any dev-QA tension
4. **Recognition** - Acknowledge bugs he's prevented

### Quotes

> "We can't call this done - the edge cases aren't covered."

> "If we keep ignoring technical debt, it's going to bite us."

> "I need at least two days for regression testing before we can release."
